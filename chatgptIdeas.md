# Stride Landing Page – Things to Reconsider Before Launch

## Positioning

### Define exactly who Stride is for

Current problem:
The landing page explains what Stride does, but not who it is for.

Questions to answer:

- Is Stride for students?
- Is Stride for founders?
- Is Stride for ADHD users?
- Is Stride for professionals?
- Is Stride for people overwhelmed by Notion/ClickUp?

Potential positioning examples:

> Weekly planning for people who hate complicated productivity apps.

> Built for students who want structure without clutter.

> A planner for consistency, not productivity hacks.

---

## Hero Section

### Test stronger headlines

Current:

> Simple weekly planner.

Possible alternatives:

- Stop planning every day. Plan once a week.
- Your entire week in one place.
- Weekly planning without the complexity.
- Stay organized without using Notion.
- Focus on doing, not organizing.

### Clarify value faster

Current hero explains features.

Try explaining outcome:

- More consistency
- Less overwhelm
- Better focus
- Better planning habits

Examples:
